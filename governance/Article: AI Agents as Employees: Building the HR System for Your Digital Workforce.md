# AI Agents as Employees: Building the HR System for Your Digital Workforce

Matt Einig (Building the AI Governance Layer for Microsoft) | July 25, 2025 | [LinkedIn Post](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/ai-agents-employees-building-hr-system-your-digital-workforce-einig-1cmkf/)

Short summary:
The post says companies should treat AI agents like digital employees, not just tools, because they now handle tasks, data, and decisions with growing autonomy. Its main point is that organizations need an HR-style governance system for AI agents to control risk, cost, ownership, and accountability as adoption scales.

Practical tips derived from the post:
- Keep an inventory of every agent and assign a clear owner.
- Require approval before an agent goes into production.
- Reapprove agents when their permissions, integrations, or capabilities change.
- Monitor risk, usage, and cost regularly.
- Run access reviews and alerts for sensitive or high-impact agents.
- Offboard agents properly by revoking permissions, removing linked automations, and updating records.

In one line: AI agents need the same lifecycle management as employees, from onboarding to performance review to retirement.
